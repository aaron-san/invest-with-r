Reading a 10-K

Every year public companies in the U.S. are required by law to produce a financial report containing financial data, risk disclosures, and related information. Over the years, 10-Ks appear to be growing more complex as companies dump more and more information into them. This is partly done to reduce the risk that investors or other stakeholders will accuse the company of failing to disclose relevant and material information. Meanwhile, investors have to wain through dozens of pages of dense information trying to glean out the critical pieces.

I am a big fan of Aswath Damadoran, professor of finance at the Stern School of Business at New York University. He produced a video about reading 10-Ks which you can see here. I will discuss some of the main points which I will use when analyzing stocks in subsequent posts.
